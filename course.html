English Speaking Course Levels

  • Beginner (Level 1): Introduction to basic English vocabulary, greetings, and simple sentences.
  • Elementary (Level 2): Basic grammar (simple tenses), common phrases, and everyday conversation practice.
  • Pre-Intermediate (Level 3): Expanded grammar (past and future tenses), increased vocabulary, and short presentations.
  • Intermediate (Level 4): Complex sentence structures, discussion skills, advanced vocabulary, and listening comprehension.
  • Advanced (Level 5): Professional and academic English, public speaking, presentations, and fluency-building activities.

Complete English Speaking Course - Teaching Guide & Worksheets

Course Outline

This course is designed to take students from zero to hero in English speaking. The course is divided into four levels:

  • Level 1: Beginner — Foundations (Alphabets, Greetings, Simple Sentences)
  • Level 2: Intermediate — Sentence Building and Conversations
  • Level 3: Upper Intermediate — Fluent Speaking Skills
  • Level 4: Advanced — Expert Communication
Full Course Topics (Level wise)
Level 1: Beginner
  • Introduction to English
  • Alphabet, Pronunciation & Sounds
  • Basic Greetings and Self Introduction
  • Basic Vocabulary for Daily Life
  • Simple Sentences (Subject + Verb + Object)
  • Common English Expressions
  • Introduction to Verbs
  • Basic Tenses (Present, Past, Future - Simple)
Level 2: Intermediate
  • Making Questions and Negative Sentences
  • Common Conversations (Role-plays)
  • Vocabulary Expansion (Verbs, Adjectives, Adverbs)
  • Articles (A, An, The)
  • Prepositions (In, On, At, Under)
  • Pronouns
  • Speaking about Routine
  • Storytelling (Small Stories)
  • Basic Public Speaking Practice
Level 3: Upper Intermediate
  • Tenses Deep Dive (Continuous and Perfect Tenses)
  • Modal Verbs (Can, Could, Should, Would, Must, May, Might)
  • Connectors (And, But, Because, So)
  • Intermediate Conversations
  • Debate Practice (Simple Topics)
  • Formal and Informal Speaking
  • Problem Solving Speaking
Level 4: Advanced
  • Advanced Vocabulary and Idioms
  • Voice Modulation and Pronunciation Improvement
  • Interview Preparation Speaking
  • Speech Practice
  • Group Discussions
  • Story Narration and Description
  • Fluency Building Techniques
  • Final Test and Practice
Week-by-Week Teaching Plan (4 Months)
  • Month 1: Basic Foundation
    • Week 1: Introduction + Alphabets + Sounds
    • Week 2: Greetings + Self Introduction + Vocabulary
    • Week 3: Simple Sentences
    • Week 4: Common Expressions + Basic Verbs
  • Month 2: Sentence Building and Conversations
    • Week 5: Basic Tenses (Present, Past, Future Simple)
    • Week 6: Making Questions + Negative Sentences
    • Week 7: Common Conversations (Market, Restaurant)
    • Week 8: Vocabulary Expansion + Daily Routine Speaking
  • Month 3: Speaking Fluently
    • Week 9: Articles + Prepositions
    • Week 10: Pronouns + Storytelling
    • Week 11: Basic Public Speaking
    • Week 12: Continuous Tenses + Modal Verbs
  • Month 4: Advanced Speaking and Fluency
    • Week 13: Connectors + Intermediate Conversations
    • Week 14: Formal vs Informal Speaking + Debate Practice
    • Week 15: Idioms + Voice Modulation
    • Week 16: Final Test: Mock Interviews + Speech
Daily Class Structure (1-hour class)
  • 0-10 min: Warm-up (Vocabulary/Conversation game)
  • 10-30 min: Teach Topic of the Day (Grammar/Vocab)
  • 30-50 min: Speaking Practice (Role-plays, Short talks)
  • 50-60 min: Homework Explanation + Motivation

Ready-to-Use Worksheets

Worksheet 1: Basic Greetings & Self Introduction
Fill in the blanks:
1. Hello! My name is _________.
2. I am from _________.
3. I am a _________.
4. Good morning / Good evening. How are you?
5. I am fine, thank _________.

Practice: Introduce yourself in 3 sentences (Write or Speak):
______________________________________________________________
______________________________________________________________
Worksheet 2: Simple Sentences & Verbs
Make sentences using these words:
- I / eat / apple
- She / go / school
- They / play / cricket
- We / read / book

Write Negative Sentences:
- I like tea. -> _________________________________________________
- He plays football. -> __________________________________________
Worksheet 3: Articles: 'a', 'an', 'the'
Fill in the blanks with 'a', 'an', or 'the':
1. I have ___ apple.
2. She is reading ___ book.
3. I saw ___ elephant at the zoo.
4. There is ___ cat under the table.
5. I want ___ orange.
Worksheet 4: Prepositions Practice
Fill in the blanks with the correct preposition (in, on, at, under):
1. The book is ___ the table.
2. She lives ___ Mumbai.
3. The cat is ___ the chair.
4. I will meet you ___ 5 PM.
5. The pen is ___ the bag.
Worksheet 5: Making Questions
Change the sentences into questions:
1. You like mangoes. -> ___________________________________
2. He goes to school. -> ___________________________________
3. They play cricket. -> ___________________________________
4. She eats an apple. -> ___________________________________
